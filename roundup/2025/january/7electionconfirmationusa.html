January 6, 2025: Electoral Certification Day

On January 6, 2025, Congress gathered under unprecedented security to certify Donald Trump as the winner of the 2024 U.S. Presidential election. The peaceful certification process marked a stark contrast to the events of January 6, 2021.

Key Highlights

  • Certification: Donald Trump was certified as the winner with 312 electoral votes. Kamala Harris received 226 votes.
  • Security: Layers of tall black fencing surrounded the Capitol, while armed security escorted the electoral certificates into the chamber.
  • No Objections: Unlike in 2021, the process concluded without objections or controversy.
  • Harris’s Role: Vice President Kamala Harris presided over the session and certified her own defeat, much like Al Gore in 2001.

Timeline of Events (GMT)

  1. 4:28 PM: Congress convened under heavy snowfall to begin the certification process.
  2. 6:04 PM: Armed security escorted the mahogany boxes containing electoral certificates into the Capitol.
  3. 6:10 PM: Vice President Kamala Harris began presiding over the counting of votes.
  4. 6:30 PM: The tally was announced: Donald Trump (312 votes), Kamala Harris (226 votes).
  5. 6:32 PM: Harris declared the session dissolved, concluding the process without incident.

Reflections from Leaders

Significant Changes in the Process

The Electoral Count Act was amended, now requiring one-fifth of lawmakers in both chambers to raise objections to state results, reducing the chances of challenges disrupting the certification process. This change ensured a smoother and more orderly session.

Security Measures

The Capitol was under the highest security level since the 2021 riot. Law enforcement, including Capitol Police and the National Guard, ensured a peaceful day. The atmosphere, however, remained tense, with layers of fencing and no tourists allowed on Capitol grounds.
