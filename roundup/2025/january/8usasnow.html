US Winter Storm Claims Lives and Prompts Emergency Declarations

A severe winter storm continues to impact the United States, resulting in five fatalities and leading seven states to declare a state of emergency. The storm, driven by a polar vortex, has brought heavy snowfall, dangerous road conditions, and widespread disruptions across the country.

States Declaring Emergencies

The governors of Maryland, Missouri, Arkansas, Kansas, Virginia, West Virginia, and Kentucky have declared states of emergency in response to the storm. Authorities have urged residents to stay off the roads as conditions worsen.

"We urge everyone to stay off the roads and prioritize safety during this severe weather event."

Wes Moore, Governor of Maryland

Fatalities and Accidents

  • A man in Missouri's Jackson County suffered a fatal injury while clearing snow.
  • Two individuals in Kansas lost their lives in a vehicle accident caused by icy roads.
  • In Texas, a man's body was found at a bus shelter, suspected to have died from the cold.
  • A driver in southeast Virginia lost control of his truck, with alcohol and weather cited as contributing factors.

National Guard Deployed

The National Guard has been deployed to assist stranded motorists and emergency services in Indiana, Kansas, and western Nebraska. In Indiana alone, over 120 guardsmen are working to provide support as snow continues to cover highways shortly after being plowed.

More than 600 motorists were stranded in Missouri, while hundreds of accidents were reported in Indiana, Kansas, and Kentucky.

Power Outages and Flight Disruptions

The storm has caused widespread power outages, with more than 190,000 customers without electricity as of Tuesday morning. At its peak, nearly 320,000 homes were affected.

Flight cancellations and delays have also disrupted travel plans, with over 2,300 flights canceled and 9,200 flights delayed as of Monday.

Impact of the Polar Vortex

The polar vortex, typically confined to the Arctic region, has pushed southward, creating sub-zero temperatures and severe weather conditions across much of North America. States from the Rocky Mountains to Maine are experiencing heavy snow and ice.

Community Resilience

Despite the severe weather, some communities have found creative ways to cope. In Washington DC, residents gathered in Meridian Hill Park for a massive snowball fight, demonstrating resilience and solidarity during challenging times.
