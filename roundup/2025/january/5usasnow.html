Severe Winter Storm Puts Much of the USA on High Alert

Millions of Americans are bracing for a massive winter storm expected to bring the heaviest snowfall and coldest temperatures in over a decade. A state of emergency has been declared in multiple states. Below are the latest updates:

Weather Alerts and States of Emergency

A state of emergency has been declared in Kentucky, Virginia, Kansas, Arkansas, and Missouri. Additionally, parts of the US unaccustomed to severe cold, such as Mississippi and Florida, are warned of treacherous conditions.

Major Impacts and Travel Disruptions

The storm is expected to cause extremely hazardous conditions, with black ice, whiteouts, and road closures across the Midwest and central US. Airlines such as American, Delta, Southwest, and United are waiving ticket change fees due to anticipated disruptions.

Poor weather has already caused crashes and deteriorating road conditions, with authorities urging residents to avoid travel unless necessary.

Expected Temperatures

  • Kansas: Wind chills as low as -20°F (-29°C), average low is 21°F (-6°C).
  • Louisville, Kentucky: Temperatures as low as 2°F (-16°C), down from an average low of 26°F (-3°C).
  • Richmond, Virginia: Temperatures expected to plummet to 13°F (-10°C), usually 28°F (-2°C).
  • Arkansas: As low as 21°F (-6°C), usually 33°F (1°C).
  • St. Louis, Missouri: Forecasted 3°F (-16°C), significantly below the average low of 23°F (-5°C).

Historical Context

This could be the coldest January in the US since 2011, when average temperatures dropped to 30°F (-1°C), making it the coolest January since 1994. The cold temperatures and snowfalls recorded in 2011 broke records in cities like New York City and Hartford, Connecticut.

The Polar Vortex Effect

The extreme weather is being caused by the polar vortex, an area of cold air that usually circulates around the Arctic but has expanded over the US. This has brought snow, ice, and sub-zero temperatures to regions unaccustomed to such conditions.
